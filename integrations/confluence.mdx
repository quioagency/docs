Connecting Confluence Cloud gives Company Brain access to the documentation, runbooks, and institutional knowledge your team maintains in Atlassian's wiki platform. After authorizing via Atlassian OAuth, you select which spaces to include, and Company Brain begins indexing pages and blog posts — making them instantly queryable from Company Brain or from Slack.

## Supported Versions

Company Brain supports **Confluence Cloud** only. Confluence Data Center and Confluence Server (self-hosted) are not currently supported. If your organization uses a self-hosted Confluence deployment, contact [support@companybrain.com](mailto:support@companybrain.com) to be notified when Data Center support becomes available.

## What Gets Indexed

Company Brain indexes the following Confluence content:

- **Pages** — Full text of all pages in selected spaces, including nested child pages
- **Blog posts** — All blog posts published within selected spaces
- **Space descriptions** — The short description text configured for each space
- **Comments** (optional) — Page and blog post comments can be included or excluded in **Sources → Confluence → Settings**

## What Is Not Indexed

The following content types are not indexed:

- File attachments (PDFs, images, Office files attached to pages)
- Macros that render content from external sources (e.g., Jira issue lists, embedded iframes)
- Restricted pages that the connected Atlassian user account does not have permission to view
- Archived spaces

## Connect Confluence to Company Brain

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Confluence source settings">
    In Company Brain, navigate to **Settings → Sources → Add Source**, then select **Confluence** from the list of available sources.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your Confluence site URL">
    In the connection form, enter your Confluence Cloud site URL. This is typically in the format:
    ```
    yourcompany.atlassian.net
    ```
    Do not include `https://` or a trailing slash.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize via Atlassian OAuth">
    Click **Connect with Atlassian**. You'll be redirected to Atlassian's OAuth consent screen. Log in with the Atlassian account you want to use for indexing, review the requested permissions, and click **Accept**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select spaces to index">
    After authorization, Company Brain fetches the list of Confluence spaces accessible to the connected account. Select the spaces you want to include in your knowledge base. You can select individual spaces or all spaces at once.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start indexing">
    Click **Start Indexing**. Company Brain begins processing the selected spaces immediately. Track progress in **Sources → Confluence** — you'll see page counts and estimated completion time as indexing proceeds.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Managing Spaces

You can add or remove Confluence spaces from your index at any time. Navigate to **Sources → Confluence → Edit Spaces** to update your selection. Adding a new space queues all its pages for indexing on the next cycle. Removing a space stops future syncs for that space, and its content is removed from the knowledge base at the next sync.

## Page Restrictions and Permissions

Company Brain fully respects Confluence page-level restrictions. Only pages that the connected Atlassian user account can view are indexed. If a page has a "View" restriction set to specific users or groups, and the connected account is not included, that page is silently skipped.

This means the connected account's permission level determines what gets indexed. For complete coverage, use an account that has viewer access to all spaces and pages you intend to include.

<Note>
  Confluence Cloud **free plans** are subject to Atlassian API rate limits, which can significantly slow initial indexing for large spaces. If you're on a free plan and indexing a space with thousands of pages, expect the initial index to take several hours. Subsequent syncs are incremental and much faster.
</Note>

## Sync Frequency

Company Brain syncs connected Confluence spaces every **24 hours** by default. Each sync fetches pages updated since the last sync, indexes new pages, and removes pages that have been deleted or archived. To run an immediate sync, click **Sync Now** in **Sources → Confluence**.

<Accordion title="What happens when a page is updated in Confluence?">
  On the next scheduled or manual sync, Company Brain fetches the updated version of the page and re-indexes it. The previous version is replaced in the knowledge base, so answers will always reflect the most recently published content.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Are Confluence page hierarchies preserved?">
  Company Brain indexes each page as an individual document and records its position in the page hierarchy as metadata. When surfacing answers, source links include the full page path (e.g., **Engineering → Runbooks → Deploy Process**) so your team can quickly locate the original content in Confluence.
</Accordion>

<Tip>
  Start by indexing one or two high-value spaces — such as your **Engineering** or **Product** space — before enabling your entire Confluence instance. This lets you validate answer quality and tune your configuration before scaling up. You can always add more spaces from **Edit Spaces**.
</Tip>

<Warning>
  Removing the Confluence source from **Settings → Sources** permanently deletes all indexed Confluence content from your Company Brain knowledge base. This action cannot be undone. Reconnecting will require a full re-index of all selected spaces.
</Warning>

The Slack integration turns Company Brain into an always-available resource inside the communication tool your team already uses. Once connected, a Company Brain bot joins your Slack workspace and responds to questions from any channel it's been invited to — returning answers, confidence levels, and direct links to source documents, all within the conversation thread.

## What the Slack Integration Does

The integration adds a **Company Brain bot** to your Slack workspace. Team members interact with it using the `/brain ask` slash command, available in any channel or direct message. You can invite the bot to specific channels where questions are likely to come up — support channels, onboarding channels, or team-specific help desks. The bot does not read or index your Slack message history; it only responds to explicit `/brain ask` commands.

## Set Up the Slack Integration

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Slack integration settings">
    In Company Brain, navigate to **Settings → Integrations → Slack**, then click **Connect**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize Company Brain in Slack">
    You'll be redirected to Slack's OAuth authorization screen. Review the requested permissions, then click **Allow**. Company Brain requests only the permissions it needs to post messages and respond to slash commands.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the bot has been added">
    After authorization, you'll be redirected back to Company Brain. A confirmation message confirms the Company Brain bot has been added to your Slack workspace. You'll also see a welcome message from the bot in your Slack **#general** channel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Invite the bot to relevant channels">
    The bot is not added to any channels automatically. In each Slack channel where you want it available, type:
    ```text
    /invite @CompanyBrain
    ```
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ask your first question">
    With the bot in a channel, any team member can ask a question using the slash command:
    ```text
    /brain ask What is our vacation policy?
    ```
    The bot will reply in a thread within a few seconds.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Usage Examples

Use `/brain ask` followed by any question in plain English. Here are some examples of what your team can ask:

```text
/brain ask What is the engineering on-call rotation?
```

```text
/brain ask How do I submit an expense report?
```

```text
/brain ask Who is the contact for IT equipment requests?
```

```text
/brain ask What are the steps for deploying to production?
```

## Answer Format

When the bot responds, it posts a **threaded reply** containing three elements:

- **Answer** — A plain-English response synthesized from your indexed knowledge base
- **Confidence** — A label (High, Medium, or Low) indicating how strongly the indexed content supports the answer
- **Source link** — A direct link to the source document (Notion page, Google Doc, Confluence page, or uploaded file) where the answer was found

If the answer draws from multiple sources, all relevant sources are listed.

## Escalation Behavior

If Company Brain cannot find a confident answer in the knowledge base, it doesn't guess. Instead, it posts a message in the channel acknowledging the question couldn't be answered, and simultaneously sends a notification to your configured **escalation channel** (set in **Settings → Escalation**) so a team member can follow up. This ensures no question goes unanswered.

<Tip>
  Invite the Company Brain bot to your **#onboarding** and **#help** channels first. These channels are where new hires and teammates are most likely to ask recurring questions — exactly the questions your knowledge base is built to answer.
</Tip>

## Remove the Slack Integration

To disconnect Company Brain from your Slack workspace, navigate to **Settings → Integrations → Slack** and click **Disconnect**. This removes the bot from your workspace and revokes all permissions. Existing answers already posted in Slack threads are not deleted.

<Warning>
  Disconnecting the Slack integration immediately disables the `/brain ask` command for all users in your workspace. If you plan to reconnect later, you'll need to re-invite the bot to all channels.
</Warning>

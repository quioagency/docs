Getting a great answer from Company Brain starts with knowing how to ask. The assistant is designed to handle natural language — you don't need Boolean operators or exact keyword matches — but a little context and specificity go a long way. This page walks you through where to ask questions, how to phrase them well, and how to read the answers you get back.

## Where to Ask

Company Brain meets you in the tools you're already using. Choose the channel that fits your workflow:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Web App">
    The web app is the most full-featured interface. The question bar sits at the top of the screen on every page — click it, type your question, and press **Enter**. The web app supports multi-turn conversations, so you can ask follow-up questions in the same thread and the assistant will maintain context across the exchange.

    Best for exploratory questions, detailed research, and any time you want to review source documents in depth.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Slack">
    Invite the Company Brain bot to any channel or DM, then use the slash command:

    ```text
    /brain ask What is our parental leave policy?
    ```

    The bot replies in the thread with a summary answer and links to the source documents. For longer answers, it posts a "View full answer" link that opens in the web app.

    Best for quick lookups during meetings, async standups, or any time you need a fast answer without switching apps.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="API">
    Use the REST API to embed Company Brain answers in your own tools, workflows, or internal dashboards.

    ```bash
    curl -X POST https://api.companybrain.io/api/v1/search \
      -H "Authorization: Bearer YOUR_API_KEY" \
      -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
      -d '{
        "question": "What are the API rate limits for our platform?",
        "workspace_id": "YOUR_WORKSPACE_ID"
      }'
    ```

    The response includes the answer text, confidence score, and an array of source citations. See the [API reference](/api/search) for the full response schema and available parameters.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Tips for Better Answers

<Accordion title="Be specific about what you need">
  Specific questions produce sharper answers. Compare these two:

  - ❌ "Tell me about incidents"
  - ✅ "What is the SLA for P1 incidents?"

  The second version gives the assistant a concrete target. When your question is broad, the assistant has to guess which aspect matters most, and the answer may be too general to be useful.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Include context when it matters">
  If your question applies to a particular segment, product, or situation, say so. The assistant will prioritize documents that match that context.

  - ❌ "What is our data retention policy?"
  - ✅ "For enterprise customers, what is our data retention policy?"

  Adding context like customer tier, region, team, or product line helps the assistant surface the right document when multiple policies exist.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Use follow-up questions in the same thread">
  You don't have to pack everything into one question. In the web app, the assistant maintains context across a conversation thread — ask a broad question first, then drill in:

  1. "What is our onboarding process for new engineers?"
  2. "What tools do they need access to in the first week?"
  3. "Who is responsible for provisioning GitHub access?"

  Each follow-up builds on the previous answer, so you get progressively more specific information without repeating yourself.
</Accordion>

<Tip>
  Use the **web app** for exploratory questions where you want to browse sources and ask follow-ups. Use **Slack** for quick, single-question lookups during meetings or while you're in the middle of another task — the bot answers in the thread without breaking your flow.
</Tip>

## Understanding the Answer Format

Every response from the assistant follows a consistent structure so you can quickly scan for what you need and assess how much to trust the answer.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Answer Text">
    A direct, plain-language response to your question. The assistant synthesizes the relevant passages into a coherent answer rather than quoting documents verbatim, so it reads naturally and gets straight to the point.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confidence Score">
    Rated **High**, **Medium**, or **Low** — this reflects how strongly the retrieved evidence supports the answer.

    - **High** — multiple sources corroborate the answer clearly.
    - **Medium** — relevant content was found, but it was partial or slightly ambiguous.
    - **Low** — the assistant found something related but isn't confident it fully addresses your question. Consider verifying with the source or escalating.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Source Citations">
    A list of the documents the answer was drawn from, each displaying the document name, source (e.g., Notion, Google Drive), and a direct link. Click any citation to open the original document in full context.
  </Step>
  <Step title='"Not Found" Response'>
    When no relevant content exists in your knowledge base, the assistant returns a clear "I don't have enough information to answer this" message rather than guessing. You'll see an option to escalate the question to a team member who can provide a verified answer.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  A **Low** confidence score means the assistant is uncertain — treat the answer as a starting point, not a definitive source. Always check the cited documents directly when accuracy is critical, such as for compliance, legal, or security questions.
</Warning>

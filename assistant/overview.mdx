The Company Brain AI assistant lets you ask questions about your organization's knowledge in plain English — no search queries, no digging through folders, no guessing which document contains the answer. The assistant reads across every connected source (Notion, Google Drive, Slack, Confluence, and more), retrieves the most relevant content, and synthesizes a direct answer with links to the original documents so you can verify every claim.

## Key Capabilities

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Natural Language Q&A" icon="comments">
    Ask questions the way you'd ask a colleague — full sentences, casual phrasing, or precise terminology. The assistant understands intent, not just keywords, so you get relevant answers even when your wording doesn't exactly match the source material.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Source Citations" icon="bookmark">
    Every answer includes references to the exact documents it drew from. Each citation links directly to the source so you can read the full context, confirm accuracy, or share the original with a teammate.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Escalation" icon="arrow-up-right">
    When the assistant can't find a confident answer, it doesn't guess. Instead, it routes the question to a designated person or Slack channel so your team can respond — and optionally add the answer back to the knowledge base.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Multi-Channel Access" icon="layer-group">
    Use the assistant wherever work happens: the Company Brain web app, the Slack bot (`/brain ask`), or programmatically via the REST API. Your knowledge base is always one question away, regardless of the tool you're in.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How Answers Are Generated

When you submit a question, the assistant follows a three-stage process to produce a grounded, reliable answer:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Search">
    The assistant runs a semantic search across all indexed content in your workspace, identifying documents, pages, and messages that are most relevant to your question.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Retrieve">
    The most relevant passages are pulled from those documents. The assistant weighs recency, relevance score, and source authority to select the content that best addresses your question.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Synthesize">
    The assistant composes a direct, human-readable answer from the retrieved passages. It does not embellish, infer beyond the evidence, or draw on information from outside your knowledge base.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The assistant is scoped entirely to your workspace's knowledge base. It does not access the internet, external databases, or any content outside of your connected and indexed sources. If the answer isn't in your knowledge base, the assistant will tell you so.
</Note>

## Confidence and Citations

Every answer the assistant returns includes two trust signals to help you evaluate the response:

- **Confidence indicator** — Rated **High**, **Medium**, or **Low** based on how strongly the retrieved evidence supports the answer. A High confidence score means multiple relevant passages corroborated the same conclusion. A Low score means the evidence was sparse or only partially related.
- **Source citations** — A list of the documents and pages the answer was drawn from, each linking directly to the original. Use these to read the full context or verify details the assistant summarized.

## What the Assistant Won't Do

The assistant is intentionally constrained to protect answer quality:

<Accordion title="It won't hallucinate answers">
  The assistant only states what it can support with content from your knowledge base. If the retrieved evidence is insufficient to form a reliable answer, it returns an "I don't know" response rather than filling in gaps with plausible-sounding fabrications.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="It won't answer from outside your knowledge base">
  General internet knowledge, training data, and external sources are not used. Every answer traces back to a document in your workspace. This keeps answers relevant to your organization's specific policies, processes, and context.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="It won't stay silent about uncertainty">
  When confidence is low or no relevant content is found, the assistant says so clearly and offers to escalate the question to a team member who can provide a verified answer.
</Accordion>

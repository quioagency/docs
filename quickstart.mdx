title: "Quickstart: Get Your First Answer from Company Brain"
sidebarTitle: "Quick Start"
description: "Create your account, connect a knowledge source, and get your first AI-generated answer with source citations — all in under five minutes."
---

This guide walks you through everything you need to go from zero to your first answer. By the end, you'll have a connected knowledge source, an indexed knowledge base, and a real response from the Company Brain AI assistant — both through the UI and the REST API.

<Steps>

  <Step title="Create Your Account">
    Navigate to [app.companybrain.ai/signup](https://app.companybrain.ai/signup) and enter your work email address and a password. Company Brain will send a verification email to the address you provided.

    Open the email and click **Verify my email** to activate your account. If the email doesn't arrive within two minutes, check your spam folder or click **Resend verification email** on the signup page.

    <Info>
      Using Google or Microsoft SSO? Click **Continue with Google** or **Continue with Microsoft** on the signup page to skip the email verification step entirely.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a Workspace">
    After verifying your email, you'll be prompted to create your first workspace. A workspace is the container for your organization's knowledge — it holds your connected sources, indexed content, members, and settings.

    1. Enter a **Workspace Name**. Use your company or team name (for example, `Acme Corp` or `Product Team`).
    2. Select a **Plan**. Choose the Free plan to explore Company Brain with up to three sources, or select a paid plan to unlock unlimited sources and advanced features.
    3. Click **Create Workspace**.

    You'll land on the workspace dashboard, where you can manage sources, run queries, and invite teammates.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Connect Your First Source">
    Company Brain pulls knowledge from the tools your team already uses. In this step, you'll connect Google Drive as your first knowledge source.

    1. In the left sidebar, click **Sources**.
    2. Click **Add Source** in the top-right corner.
    3. Select **Google Drive** from the integration catalogue.
    4. Click **Authenticate with Google** and sign in with the Google account that has access to the files you want to index. Grant the requested permissions — Company Brain requests read-only access.
    5. After authenticating, you'll see your Google Drive folder tree. Select the folders you want to include in your knowledge base. You can choose specific folders or select **All of My Drive**.
    6. Click **Save and Start Sync**.

    <Warning>
      Company Brain will only index files you explicitly select. Deselecting a folder later removes its content from your knowledge base during the next sync cycle.
    </Warning>

    <Tabs>
      <Tab title="What Gets Indexed">
        Company Brain indexes Google Docs, Sheets (as structured text), Slides (speaker notes and slide text), and PDFs stored in Drive. Binary files such as images, videos, and audio are skipped.
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="Permissions">
        Company Brain inherits the sharing settings of your Google Drive files. If a file is restricted to specific users, only workspace members with access to that file will receive answers sourced from it.
      </Tab>
    </Tabs>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Wait for Indexing">
    As soon as you save your source, Company Brain begins ingesting and indexing your content. You'll see a progress indicator on the **Sources** page showing the current sync status.

    - **Small workspaces** (fewer than 500 documents) typically finish indexing within two to five minutes.
    - **Large workspaces** (thousands of documents) may take up to 30 minutes for the initial index.

    You don't need to stay on the page — Company Brain sends you an email when indexing is complete. You can also refresh the **Sources** page and look for the green **Synced** badge next to your source.

    <Info>
      After the initial index, Company Brain runs incremental syncs every hour by default. Any new or updated documents appear in your knowledge base within the next sync window. You can trigger a manual sync at any time from the **Sources** page.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Ask Your First Question">
    With your source indexed, you're ready to query your knowledge base. Click the **Ask** button in the top navigation bar — or press `⌘K` (Mac) / `Ctrl+K` (Windows) — to open the question bar.

    Type a question in plain English. For example:

    ```text
    What is our refund policy?
    ```

    Press **Enter** or click the send icon. Company Brain will return an answer in seconds.

    **Understanding the answer format:**

    - **Answer text** — A direct, concise response synthesized from your knowledge base.
    - **Source citations** — Numbered references (e.g., `[1]`, `[2]`) appear inline in the answer text. Click any citation to open the source document directly.
    - **Confidence indicator** — A signal showing how strongly the answer is grounded in your indexed content. High confidence means multiple sources support the answer.
    - **Follow-up suggestions** — Related questions you might want to ask next, generated automatically from the context of your answer.

    <Note>
      If Company Brain can't find a confident answer in your knowledge base, it will tell you so explicitly rather than guessing. You can configure **Escalation** to route unanswered questions to a Slack channel or a designated team member. See [Escalation](/assistant/overview) for setup instructions.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Query the API">
    You can also query your knowledge base programmatically using the Company Brain REST API. Retrieve your API token from **Workspace Settings → API Keys**, then send a search request:

    ```bash
    curl -X POST https://api.companybrain.ai/v1/search \
      -H "Authorization: Bearer YOUR_API_TOKEN" \
      -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
      -d '{
        "query": "What is our refund policy?",
        "limit": 5
      }'
    ```

    A successful response returns an AI-generated answer along with the source documents that support it:

    ```json
    {
      "answer": "Customers may request a full refund within 30 days of purchase by contacting support@example.com.",
      "sources": [
        {
          "id": "src_abc123",
          "title": "Refund Policy — Customer Handbook",
          "url": "https://drive.google.com/file/d/...",
          "excerpt": "Full refunds are available within 30 days of the original purchase date..."
        }
      ],
      "confidence": "high"
    }
    ```

    <Tip>
      Use the `limit` parameter to control how many source documents are returned alongside the answer. The default is `5`; the maximum is `20`.
    </Tip>

    You can also list the knowledge sources connected to your workspace:

    ```bash
    curl https://api.companybrain.ai/v1/sources \
      -H "Authorization: Bearer YOUR_API_TOKEN"
    ```

    See the full [API Reference](/api/overview) for all available endpoints.
  </Step>

</Steps>

<Tip>
  Google Drive is just one of many integrations. Company Brain also connects to Notion, Slack, Confluence, SharePoint, Zendesk, and more. See the full list and setup guides in the [Integrations Overview](/integrations/overview).
</Tip>

Admins can invite team members, assign and change roles, and remove users from the **Team** settings page. Good team management keeps your workspace secure, ensures the right people have the right level of access, and maintains a clean audit trail of who has interacted with your organization's knowledge base.

## Inviting Members

### Inviting an Individual Member

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Invite Panel">
    Navigate to **Settings → Team**, then click **Invite Members** in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the Email Address">
    Type the invitee's work email address in the email field. Company Brain will warn you if the domain is outside your allowed email domains list — see [Security settings](/admin/workspace-settings#security) to manage that list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a Role">
    Choose the role this person should have: **Viewer**, **Member**, **Editor**, or **Admin**. Not sure which to pick? See the [roles and permissions table](/admin/access-control#roles-and-permissions) for a full breakdown.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send the Invite">
    Click **Send Invite**. Company Brain dispatches an invitation email immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Invitee Signs Up">
    The invitee receives an email containing a unique signup link valid for **72 hours**. Clicking the link takes them through account creation and lands them directly in your workspace with the assigned role.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Bulk Inviting Members

To invite multiple people at once, paste a comma-separated list of email addresses into the email field during the invite flow. All addresses in the batch receive the same role, so send separate batches if you need to assign different roles to different groups. There is no limit on the number of addresses per batch, subject to your plan's member limit.

<Info>
  Bulk invites count against your plan's member limit immediately, even before recipients accept. If an invite expires without being accepted, the reserved seat is released automatically.
</Info>

## Changing a Member's Role

To update someone's role after they have joined:

1. Go to **Settings → Team**.
2. Find the member using the search bar or by scrolling the member list.
3. Click the **role badge** next to their name to open the role dropdown.
4. Select the new role.

The change takes effect immediately — no confirmation step is required. The member's new permissions apply the next time they perform an action or refresh their session.

<Tip>
  Use the **Editor** role for team leads who need to connect and manage knowledge sources on behalf of their team, but who shouldn't have access to workspace-wide settings, billing, or member management. This keeps admin privileges limited to a small group while still empowering contributors.
</Tip>

## Removing a Member

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the Member">
    Go to **Settings → Team** and locate the member you want to remove.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the Options Menu">
    Click the **three-dot menu** (⋯) to the right of the member's row.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove from Workspace">
    Select **Remove from workspace** from the dropdown and confirm the action in the dialog that appears.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Removed members lose access to Company Brain **immediately** — their session is terminated and they cannot rejoin unless re-invited. Their query history, however, is retained in the workspace's audit log for compliance purposes. Removing a member does not delete their past activity.
</Warning>

## Managing Pending Invites

Invites that have not yet been accepted appear under **Settings → Team → Pending Invites**. From this view you can:

- **Resend** an invite to re-send the invitation email with a fresh 72-hour link.
- **Revoke** an invite to cancel it before the recipient accepts, freeing up the reserved member seat.

Check pending invites regularly — if a batch of invites was sent to incorrect addresses, revoke them promptly to avoid exposing your workspace link.

## SSO Provisioning *(Enterprise)*

On the Enterprise plan, members can be automatically **provisioned** (added) and **deprovisioned** (removed) through your organization's identity provider using SCIM. This means:

- New employees added to the relevant group in Okta, Azure AD, or Google Workspace are automatically invited to Company Brain with the role you've mapped in your IdP.
- Employees who leave the organization or are removed from the group lose workspace access within minutes — no manual removal required.

<Accordion title="Supported identity providers for SCIM provisioning">
  - Okta
  - Microsoft Azure Active Directory (Entra ID)
  - Google Workspace
  - Any SCIM 2.0-compatible identity provider

  For setup instructions, refer to your **Enterprise Onboarding Guide** or contact your dedicated Customer Success Manager.
</Accordion>

<Note>
  SSO provisioning is available exclusively on the Enterprise plan. If you are on a Free or Pro plan and need automated member management, contact sales to discuss an upgrade.
</Note>
